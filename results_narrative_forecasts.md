# Narrative Forecast Accuracy — Results & Specification

This document describes what the narrative forecast code must implement, how forecasts are graded, and the exact numerical results stated in the thesis. It is intended as a checklist for verifying code correctness.

---

## 1. Context and Goal

In addition to numeric rating predictions, the thesis produces **free-form narrative forecasts** — text paragraphs written from an ex-ante perspective predicting what will happen in an activity. These are graded by `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` for how similar they are to the actual post-activity outcome summary.

The goal is to identify which combination of LLM methods produces the most accurate and well-reasoned narrative description of future activity outcomes.

---

## 2. Generating the Reference: Post-Activity Outcome Summaries

Before grading, a reference text is constructed for each activity from its post-activity evaluation documents:

1. `gemini-2.5-flash` selects up to **10 pages** from post-activity documents that are:
   - Categorized as `deviation_from_plans`, `delays_or_early_completion`, or `over_or_under_spending`, with a relevance score ≥ 3/10, OR
   - Graded as highly relevant to activity evaluation (if insufficient categorized pages exist)
2. **Fallback:** If no pages meet the threshold, the first 10 pages of the highest-ranked post-activity evaluation document are used.
3. These pages are summarized by `gemini-2.5-flash` into a post-activity summary (3–10 paragraphs covering successes, shortfalls, and quantitative outcomes).

This outcome summary is the **reference text** for grading.

---

## 3. Generating Narrative Forecasts

Several LLM configurations are tested for narrative generation. All configurations operate from **pre-activity information only** (no post-activity leakage). The forecast is written from an ex-ante perspective.

### 3.1 Models Tested
- `deepseek-V3.2` — primary forecasting model (selected for benchmark performance and low cost; non-reasoning mode avoids post-cutoff leakage)
- `gemini-2.5-flash` — tested for fine-tuning potential
- `gemini-2.5-pro` (referred to as `gemini-3-pro` in some places in the thesis) — highest capability model tested

### 3.2 Prompt Components

The full prompt is assembled in this order:

1. **Activity metadata** — title, planned start/end dates, scope, planned disbursement (USD), location(s), GDP per capita, participating and implementing organisations.
2. **Baseline description & targets** — `gemini-2.5-flash`-generated: activity description, targets summary, contextual challenge, complexity, integratedness, financing summary, implementer performance context.
3. **Risk summary** — `gemini-2.5-flash`-generated structured summary of key project risks.
4. **Retrieved baseline excerpts (RAG)** — additional activity-specific information retrieved by the model from available documents and synthesised into a short passage (`{rag_synthesis_additional_info}`).
5. **KNN few-shot block** — summarised mock forecasts of the 3 most semantically similar training activities, one from each broad rating tier.
6. **Multi-stage reasoning (S1/S2)** — outputs of two prior LLM calls:
   - S1: reasons the activity may have been rated "Moderately Satisfactory" or **worse**
   - S2: reasons it may have been rated "Moderately Satisfactory" or **better**
7. **Rating distribution prior** — empirical distribution of training-set rating outcomes (to reduce mode-collapse).

### 3.3 KNN Few-Shot Block Details

- Similarity based on `gemini-embedding-001` semantic embeddings of the LLM-generated targets text.
- **15 nearest neighbors** were found optimal for weighting (tested 1, 3, 7, 10, 15, 20).
- For the few-shot block: **k=3** summarized mock forecasts (tested 1, 5, 7; k=3 best).
- One example from each rating tier: low (1–3), mid (4), high (5–6).
- Each example includes: key metadata (title, location, brief summary), risk summary, retrospective mock forecast analysis, and final outcome label.

### 3.4 Mock Forecast Generation (Training Data)

Mock forecasts for training examples are generated by `gemini-2.5-pro` using:
- Up to 10 baseline pages (from forecast-informative categories with relevance ≥ 9)
- Up to 10 outcome/evaluation pages (prioritizing deviation/delays/spending, relevance ≥ 3)
- Activity metadata + model-generated baseline summaries
- Prompt instructs the model to write from ex-ante perspective AND end by stating the **known** final evaluation rating (from the merged ratings file)
- Returns `NO RESPONSE` if evaluation excerpts are insufficient

### 3.5 Forced Rating Method (RF-Forced)

In addition to free-choice forecasts, an **RF-forced** variant is tested where the LLM is instructed to conclude with a specific rating derived from the statistical model's prediction. This is done by injecting e.g. `"Conclude with a forecast of moderately satisfactory"` based on the RF+ET output.

This is found to be the **best method** for narrative similarity grades.

### 3.6 Response Format

The model is required to produce a structured response:
1. Reasons the outcome may be rated `{midpoint_low_text}` or lower
2. Reasons it may be rated `{midpoint_high_text}` or higher
3. Aggregate considerations and select exactly one of `{num_options}` outcomes
4. Final line: `FORECAST: ` followed by the chosen option

All LLMs are instructed to "think like a superforecaster" (per Tetlock & Gardner, 2015).

## 4. Grading Narrative Forecasts

### 4.1 Grader Model and Inputs
- Grader: `gemini-2.5-flash-lite`
- Input: the narrative forecast text + the reference post-activity outcome summary
- Grading criteria: two key dimensions:
  1. Accurate identification of likely **drivers** of activity success or failure
  2. Accurate identification of likely **outcomes**

### 4.2 Grading Scale

US letter-grade scheme converted to numeric scores:
- F = 55 or lower (completely wrong or unsupported)
- D+/D/D- ≈ 57–66 (mostly inaccurate, wrong focus)
- C+/C/C- ≈ 67–76 (partially accurate; major outcomes incorrect, some smaller aspects correct)
- B+/B/B- ≈ 77–86 (mostly accurate; at least one major outcome forecasted, at least one key driver identified)
- A+/A/A- ≈ 87–97 (excellent; highly accurate, multiple major events forecasted)

Grades span from approximately 55 to 97, in even intervals between letter grades.

### 4.3 Baseline for Comparison
- **Risks-only baseline:** `gemini-2.5-flash` summary of project risks from pre-activity documents submitted to the grader (instead of a proper narrative forecast).

---

## 5. LLM Configuration Nomenclature

The thesis reports results for all combinations of:
- **Model:** `deepseek-V3.2`, `gemini-2.5-flash`, `gemini-2.5-pro` (`gemini-3-pro`)
- **Context additions:**
  - `KNN`: 3 few-shot similar examples included
  - `RAG`: model retrieves additional information it decided it was missing from the available documents
  - `S1`: additional reasoning for "why outcome may be unsatisfactory" injected
  - `S2`: additional reasoning for "why outcome may be satisfactory" injected
- **RF-forced:** LLM instructed to match the statistical model's forecast

All combinations are evaluated on **n=300 activities** (the 300 latest-starting training activities, same as used for LLM Adjustment ridge regression calibration, plus the full validation set of 268 activities).

The comparison figure (Figure `scatterLLMcorrected`) shows validation set results for all configurations as scatter plots:
- X-axis: mean narrative grade (55–97 scale)
- Y-axis (top): R² between predicted and true ratings
- Y-axis (bottom): within-group pairwise ranking

## 6. Key Numerical Results

| Configuration | Narrative Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **RF-forced, full context (RAG+KNN+S1+S2)** | **88.5** (`\BestNarrativeGrade`) | Best narrative similarity; corresponds to B+ |
| Risks-only baseline | 80.2 (`\RisksOnlyGrade`) | Between C+ and B-; no affirmative outcome forecasts |
| deepseek-V3.2 (no additional methods) | ~B (≈83) | Baseline LLM; risks summary gives C+/B- |
| gemini-3-pro or RF-forced | ~B+ (≈85–88) | Can lift to B+ range |
| Minimal-prompt baseline | Worst narrative similarity | But highest R² for rating prediction |

**Primary result:** Best narrative grade = **88.5** (B+), achieved by RF-forced with RAG+KNN+S1+S2.

**Key findings:**
- Rating accuracy and narrative quality are **inversely correlated** in some configurations: the minimal-prompt baseline achieves the highest R² but worst narrative similarity.
- **RF-forced methods dominate** on both narrative grade and within-group pairwise ranking.
- RAG, KNN, S1, and S2 consistently improve narrative similarity grades, but by a small margin.
- Better models (gemini-3-pro) achieve better narrative quality than weaker models.
- No consistent difference found between `deepseek-V3.2` and `gemini-2.5-flash` in forecasting skill.

## 7. Fine-Tuning Results

- Model fine-tuned: `gemini-2.5-flash`
- Objective: DPO (Direct Preference Optimization, intended to improve within-group pairwise ranking)
- Training loss: reduced by 95% over the fine-tuning run
- Tested on: early subset of validation data (excluding BMZ activities)
- **Results:**
  - R² improved modestly but remained worse than predicting the mean rating
  - Far below performance of `gemini-3-pro`
  - Within-group pairwise ranking slightly **worsened** by fine-tuning
  - Cost: ~140 EUR for 20 epochs over 50 pairs
- **Conclusion:** Fine-tuning at this scale is not a promising strategy.

---

## 8. Information Leakage in LLM Forecasts

- Validated on validation set: no significant leakage detected.
- Validated on test set: **significant future leakage detected** — LLM reasoning cited COVID-19 and conflict events occurring during the activity period.
- GPT-3.5-turbo and GPT-4 show absolute error increase for activities concluding after their training cutoff dates; this decline is **not observed in statistical models**.
- `deepseek-V3.2` with thinking enabled outperforms RF+ET on within-group pairwise ranking, but this is attributed at least in part to leakage.
- LLM forecasts on the test set should be treated with caution.

## 9. Verification Checklist for Code

- [ ] Post-activity outcome summary generated from pages with relevance ≥ 3/10 in deviation/delays/spending categories, or highest-relevance evaluation pages as fallback
- [ ] Narrative forecast prompt follows 7-block structure (metadata, description, risks, RAG, KNN, S1+S2, rating distribution)
- [ ] KNN block: k=3 examples, one per rating tier (low/mid/high), based on gemini-embedding-001 semantic similarity
- [ ] S1 and S2 reasoning steps generated as separate prior LLM calls and injected into final prompt
- [ ] Rating distribution prior appended to prevent mode-collapse
- [ ] "Think like a superforecaster" instruction included in all prompts
- [ ] RF-forced variant: inject statistical model's predicted rating label into prompt
- [ ] Mock forecasts generated by `gemini-2.5-pro` using ≤10 baseline + ≤10 evaluation pages; forced to end with known rating
- [ ] Grading uses `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` against post-activity outcome summary
- [ ] Grading evaluates drivers and outcomes separately
- [ ] Grade scale: F≈55, A+≈97, even intervals between letter grades
- [ ] n=300 activities evaluated for LLM comparison (latest-starting training + full validation)
- [ ] Risks-only baseline: submit risk summary (not forecast) to grader for comparison
- [ ] Figure: scatter plot of narrative grade (x) vs. R² (y-top) and pairwise ranking (y-bottom), validation set
